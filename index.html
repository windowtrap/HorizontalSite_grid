"In the moment of my own walk, the electronically driven soundwaves of Haraway’s voice provoked me to pause and attune to the season’s gasp for life around me—I try to imagine it, as she had experienced, as an all connected electrochemical and socio-political event unfolding around me. Electrochemical events, as Haraway alludes to, are the process through which chemicals pass though neural networks in plants, animals and humans alike enabling them to think, move, listen and change, not to mention many other markers of life.

"Haraway outlines that the cyborg is an entity bound in irony and blasphemy by being the ‘illegitimate’ progeny of militaristic technological determinism while also being an indeterminant “hybrid of machine and organism, a creature of social reality as well as a creature of fiction.”
